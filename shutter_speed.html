Shutter Speed

Shutter speed is the length of time the camera shutter is open, exposing light onto the camera sensor. Shutter speed is responsible for changing the brightness of a photo and creating dramatic effects by either freezing action or blurring motion.
 Shutter speed ranges from the fastest 1/4000 second to the slowest 30 seconds. Among all, 1/2 to 1/1000 are more commonly used.
