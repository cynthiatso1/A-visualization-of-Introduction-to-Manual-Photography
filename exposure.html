Exposure

In photography, exposure is the amount of light per unit area reaching a photographic film or electronic image sensor, as determined by shutter speed, lens aperture and scene luminance.

An exposure value (EV) is the exposure settings on your camera for a particular combination of a camera's shutter speed and aperture f-numbers.
A light value (LV) is a measure of the luminance of a scene.
Luminance is the amount of light reflecting off of the subject.And illumination is the amount of light falling on the subject.
Light values are linked to exposure values by way of the ISO setting of the camera.
